Kangaroos!

Facts about Kangaroos:

  • Scientific Name: Macropus

  • Kangaroos are marsupials that are indigenous to the Australian continent.

  • A kangaroo's pouch is technically called a marsupium and it performs a number of functions such as carrying their babies to nurse them.

  • Kangaroos are usually between 3 to 7 feet in height. They can weigh up to approximately 200 pounds.

  • Male kangaroos use their arms to flex and fend off rival mates.

  • While most of the human population are right handed, wild kangaroos gravitate to using their left hands for tasks like grooming and feeding.
